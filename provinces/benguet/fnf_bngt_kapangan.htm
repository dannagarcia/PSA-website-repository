History and Government

The municipality of Kapangan together with 16 other townships of Benguet were formally established by the American civil government on November 22, 1900 by virtue of Act No. 48. On August 13, 1908, Benguet in turn became a sub-province of the old Mountain Province by Act 1876. On June 18, 1966, Republic Act No. 4695 or An Act Creating the Provinces of Benguet, Mountain Province , Ifugao and Kalinga-Apayao was enacted into law. The law separated Benguet and 3 other provinces from the old Mountain Province . The law also reduced the number of Benguet townships to 13 municipalities, among them the municipality of Kapangan.

Its Land

Kapangan is located within 16o33" to 16o41" latitude and 120o29" to 120o40" longitude. It is about 29 kilometers away from the capital town of La Trinidad. It is bounded on the north by Kibungan, on the south by Tublay, on the southwest by Sablan, on the east by Atok, and on the west by the province of La Union.

The total land area of Kapangan is estimated to be 17,327 hectares or 6.68% of the total land area of the province of Benguet. The municipality's terrain is characterized by rugged mountains and hills. Rugged mountains have slopes of 50% while hills have slopes from 30%-50%. The highest elevation is 1,700 meters above sea level while the lowest level is 200 meters above the sea level.

Climate is divided into two seasons â€“ the wet and the dry. Wet seasons occur from the months of May to October while dry seasons occur November to April.

Its Inhabitants

Kapangan has a total population of 20,084 inhabitants in 3,805 households registering an annual growth rate of 0.1% covering the year 2007-2010.

Kankana-ey and Ibaloi are the major ethnolinguistic groups of the municipality.

Education and Culture

Kapangan has at present 27 schools, 19 of which are complete elementary schools. The municipality also has 3 high schools together with the offering of tertiary level courses at the Governor Bado Dangwa Agro Industrial School contribute to the elevation of the literacy rate of the municipality.

Like other municipalities in the province of Benguet , Kapangan also has the caÃ±ao or feast as an integral part of its culture. Musical instruments played during the caÃ±ao include the drum or solibao; gongs: the gangsa and pinsak; and a pair of iron bars: the palas or tiktik. Songs and chants include the day-eng and the bad-iw while native dances include the tayaw and sarong.

Its Economy

The municipality is primarily agricultural with 13,711.27 hectares or 79.1 percent of the land devoted to agriculture. Of total agricultural area, only 488.01 hectares is irrigated while 13,223.26 is rain-fed. Main crops planted are palay, cassava, camote, gabi, coffee and banana.

Tiger grass is also being grown and is used to make soft brooms. Trade and industry in the municipality are limited to small business establishments and cottage industries.
