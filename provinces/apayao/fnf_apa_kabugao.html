History and Government

Kabugao was organized into a township by the American colonial government in 1913. As per the Department of the Interior and Local Government, the municipality is “the oldest among the seven municipalities of the province of Apayao.”

Long before the establishment of an organized government, Kabugao was inhabited by the indigenous people known as the Isnag. For centuries, the Isnag had opposed and had never been fully subjugated by the Spaniards. In 1913, the Battle of Waga paved the way for peace in the locality when the Isnag were subdued by the Constabulary under the American regime. In 1914, the municipal district of Kabugao was formally created when Lt. Governor Blas Villamor of the American administration appointed Manuel Rugrug as the first township head of Kabugao.

On August 18, 1908, Apayao became a sub-province of the newly created Mountain Province pursuant to Act No. 1876. In 1916, Kabugao became the capital of the Apayao sub-province. On June 25, 1963, Kabugao became a regular municipality under Executive Order No. 42 issued by President Diosdado M. Macapagal.

On February 14, 1995, Kabugao became the capital of the new province of Apayao by virtue of Republic Act No. 7878.

Kabugao has 21 barangays: Badduat, Baliwanan, Bulu, Cabetayan, Dagara, Dibagat, Karagawan, Kumao, Laco, Lenneng, Lucab, Luttuacan, Madatag, Madduang, Magabta, Maragat, Musimut, Nagbalayan, Poblacion, Tuyangan and Waga.

Its Land

Kabugao is found in the geographic center of the province. The municipality’s total land area is 806.3 square kilometers or about one-fifth (19.6%) of the total land area of Apayao province. Kabugao is bounded by the municipalities of Luna and Pudtol to the north, Conner to the south, Flora and the province of Cagayan to the east, and Calanasan and the province of Abra to the west.

The locality is known for its dense forests and numerous mountain streams and brooks. Fauna such as deer, wild pigs, wild chickens, monkeys, squirrels, and various kinds of birds are still found within its forests.

Kabugao has a Type III climate characterized by a long wet season and a short dry season. The rainy season generally starts on July and ends in February while the dry season covers the months of March to May. December is the coldest month while April and May are the hottest months in the year.

Its Inhabitants

Majority of the local residents are Isnag while the minority consists of the Ilocano, Igorot, Tagalog and other ethnolinguistic groups.

The 2010 total population was recorded at 16,170. Comparing with the 2007 population count of 14,529, there was an increase of 11.3% in population in the three year period.

Its Economy

As a mountainous area, the municipality's main source of livelihood is upland farming. Rice is the principal product while corn and root crops are the secondary crops.

Other industries present in the municipality are rattan craft and bolo making.
