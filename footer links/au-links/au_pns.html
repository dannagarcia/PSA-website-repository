NSCB-CAR Publications

Regional Social and Economic Trends (RSET)

The RSET contains a compilation of available government statistics in time series. The data included in the RSET covers various sectors and these are disaggregated by province/city. The RSET is a useful reference material in the conduct of studies on patterns and trends of socioeconomic indicators at the regional and local level.

StatWatch

The StatWatch is an info sheet presenting the major and most common social and economic indicators for the region useful to the government and business planners and decision makers and the general public. The StatWatch is updated and released periodically or as soon as the latest data releases become available. This type of publication provides timely trends of developments.

Fact Sheet

The Fact Sheet showcases the relevant data/indicators and brief analyses, statistical concepts, methodologies, standards and other statistical developments which are not commonly known or readily available to users in the region. The Fact Sheet is disseminated to government and business planners and researchers to promote their understanding and awareness of events, etc.

Stat Trivia

The Stat Trivia is an infobit that features interesting statistics. The NSCB-CAR Stat Trivia is featured at least quarterly in the weekly regional newspaper ZIGZAG WEEKLY.

The Countryside in Figures: Province of Benguet

The Publication contains pertinent statistics/indicators comparing the performance of the thirteen (13) municipalities of the province of Benguet. It is a useful statistical portfolio for local planners, investors and other groups interested in Benguet as a venue for investment and development.

Poverty Mapping: Ifugao Province

In line with the government's poverty reduction program, the National Statistical Coordination Board (NSCB), in cooperation with the Provincial Government of Ifugao , is implementing a Poverty Mapping activity which aims to identify the poor areas in the province as measured by selected socioeconomic indicators. The maps may be useful for local executives, planners and other officials in identifying what and where poverty alleviation programs and projects may be implemented.

Cordillera Facts and Figures

Cordillera Facts and Figures gives a brief history and statistical profile of the physical, social and economic conditions of the region and its provinces. It aims to promote investments and tourism in the region.

Press Release

Brief articles/data features on the latest data/indicators, concepts, methodologies and developments are published in a column or StatCorner in local dailies to reach a wider audience and the general public to make them more informed.

Inventory of the Statistics Available in the Government

The inventory provides a profile of the statistics being generated/produced by government agencies and local government units in the region and is also used to identify data gaps and deficiencies. The accompanying statistical directory serves as a locator of various statistical personnel/organizations of government agencies and local government units in the region. It serves as a useful guide in locating information in the region to facilitate research, thus saving on time and resources in data sourcing.

NSCB-CAR Services

Technical Assistance to Government Agencies and Local Government Units

Technical assistance covers a wide range of services on data collection techniques, data management, analysis and interpretation to review and sampling design, project proposals and socioeconomic profiles. Thru this service, users will learn how to use available data and how to use them correctly.

National Statistical Information Center (NSIC)

The NSIC serves as the one-stop shop for statistical data and services in the region where data users can avail of these facilities under one roof.

Statistical Survey Review and Clearance System (SSRCS)

The SSRCS is the mechanism instituted to review and clear statistical surveys and censuses conducted by government agencies to ensure sound design and cost-effectiveness.
