The National Statistical Coordination Board

The National Statistical Coordination Board was created in 1987 by virtue of Executive Order No. 121 to serve as the Philippines' policy making and coordinating body on statistical matters in the country.

The major goal of the NSCB is to promote the independence, objectivity, integrity, responsiveness, and relevance of the Philippine Statistical System (PSS).

Its objective is to achieve the development of an orderly statistical system capable of providing timely, accurate and useful data for the planning, programming and evaluation needs of all sectors of the Philippine economy.

Powers and Functions

The powers and functions of the NSCB are defined under Section 5 of the Executive Order Number 121.

The Executive Order further provides that the decisions of the NSCB on statistical matters shall be final and executory.

Powers and Functions of the NSCB:

  1. Promote and maintain an efficient statistical system in the government;
  2. Formulate policies on all matters relating to government statistical operations;
  3. Recommend executive and legislative measures to enhance the development and efficiency of the system, including the internal structure of statistical agencies;
  4. Establish appropriate mechanisms fro statistical coordination at the regional, provincial and city levels;
  5. Approve the Philippine Statistical Development Plan;
  6. Allocate statistical responsibilities among government agencies by designating the statistics to be collected by them, including their periodicity and content;
  7. Review budgetary proposals involving statistical operations and submit an integrated budget for the PSS to the DBM;
  8. Review and clear, prior to release, all funds for statistical operations;
  9. Develop, prescribe and maintain appropriate frameworks for the improvement of statistical coordination; and
  10. Prescribe uniform standards and classification systems in government statistic

Regional Division-CAR

Functions

  • Communicate to statistical authorities in Manila the problems, needs and other requirements of data producers and users in the region;
  • Develop/operationalize socioeconomic frameworks and indicators systems that are reflective of and relevant to the region's conditions and statistical databases to respond to the needs of users for measures of economic and social development;
  • Serve as the one-stop shop for statistical services and products in the region, coordinate with the data producers, provide technical assistance to clients/data users, consult with educational institutions and continually initiate improvements in services and products offered;
  • Conduct assessment of data production and dissemination, identify gaps and deficiencies and recommend/implement activities, programs and projects for the improvement of frameworks and indicator system and to strengthen existing systems of data production, dissemination and utilization;
  • Disseminate in the region the statistical policies issued by the NSCB Executive Board and the different statistical standards and classifications system on government statistics developed by the NSCB and other mechanisms for coordinating the subnational statistical system, monitor adoption by concerned agencies, and make recommendations;
  • Provide secretariat and technical services to the Regional Statistical Coordination Committees (RSCC) - coordination of activities of the RSCC in support of its policy making and issue resolution functions; implementation of activities and other tasks identified/approved by the RSCC to address statistical issues and other concerns in the region; and interaction with data producers, providers and users to ensure consideration of statistical concerns;
  • Evaluate the technical aspects and budgetary requirements of statistical activities/projects of agencies in the region in line with the designation of statistical activities to generate critical data for decision-making in all sectors and prepare recommendations for approval of the RSCC and its endorsement to the Cordillera Executive Board (CEB) and Cordillera Regional Assembly (CRA) jointly acting as the regional development council, NSCB and the Department of Budget and Management (DBM);
  • Formulate guidelines on and implement a monitoring, evaluation, and feedback system for statistical activities/projects of agencies in the region; and
  • Perform other functions as may be assigned by the NSCB Secretary-General and as may be necessary to carry out the purposes of Executive Order No. 121.
