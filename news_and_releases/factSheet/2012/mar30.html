Women in CAR will outlive men until 2020

As projected by the National Statistics Office (NSO), women in the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR) will live longer than men by an average of 5.5 years. The life expectancy of women is 75 years while men are estimated to live until the age of 69. Life expectancy is an estimate of the average number of additional years a person can expect to live, based on the age-specific death rates for a given year. The projected life expectancies are from 1990 up to 2020.

The NSO estimates also revealed that life expectancy for the provinces of Abra, Kalinga-Apayao, Ifugao and Mt. Province is lower than the regional life expectancy for both women and men.

The women of Benguet province can outlive all other women in other parts of the region and even all the men in the region. While a woman in Benguet could celebrate her birthday until the age of 76, a woman in Kalinga-Apayao could live 3 years shorter at 73 years. Incidentally, the women in this area have the lowest life expectancy among all the women in the region.

Moreover, the men in Benguet can also live the longest among all other men in CAR with a life expectancy of 71 years while the men in Ifugao can live the shortest with a life expectancy of 67 years.

Also, Ifugao Province has the widest gap of life expectancy of women and men. The gap is more than 6 1/2 years.

No exact reasons were given why on the disparity of living long or less. But experts say that this could be due to our diet, lifestyle, government healthcare plan to its employees, stress, the environment and even technology!
