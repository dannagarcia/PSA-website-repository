Satellite Accounts

  • Satellite Accounts

The conventional System of National Accounts (SNA), which provides indicators to assess the country's economic performance, does not not cover or can not highlight the impacts of economic activities of certain specific sectors such as the environment (depletion and degradation of environmental and natural resources), health care services, tourism and others. Such shortcoming of the conventional accounts, however, is addressed by linking these various concerns to the SNA. The 1993 System of National Accounts (SNA) allows incorporation of new developmental concerns through functionally-oriented satellite accounting.

Satellite Accounting in the Region
  • Environmental and Natural Resources Accounting

    The compilation of environmental accounts ont he depletion of forest, mineral, land and soil and water resources; and the degradation of the environment brought about by agricultural activities in the region

  • Local Health Accounts

    Joint project with the Department of Health to formulate a scientific tool for the planning, formulation and assessment of provincial health care policies, services and financing; and t o develop the local data system using the local health accounts as framework
