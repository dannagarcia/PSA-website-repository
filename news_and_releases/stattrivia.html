Did you know that...

Americans outnumbered Korean tourists in CAR last year?

The Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR) welcomed 77,837 foreign tourists in 2014 according to the survey on DOT-accredited accommodation establishments. Foreign nationals from the USA comprised 13% of the total closely followed by South Korea at 12%. Rounding up the top 10 are France, Germany, Japan, Canada, Israel, United Kingdom, Australia and China.

PSA Stat Trivia is also published in the regional newspaper, "ZIGZAG WEEKLY."

Did you know that...

The PNP handled 645 cases of violence against women in the Cordilleras last year?

Tn 2014, the Women and Children Protection Deskof the Philippine National Police handled a total of 645 cases of violence against women in the Cordillera or an average of 54 cases per month. Of these cases, about 30% were filed in court, 16% were amicably settled, 12% were still under police investigation, 6% were dropped or closed while the rest were referred to the Provincial Prosecutor’s Office, concerned barangay or DSWD.

Did you know that...

One peso in 2006 is now worth only 72 centavos in the Cordilleras?

The value of the peso in the Cordillera region declined to 72 centavos in December 2014 from the previous year’s 74 centavos. This means, the value of the same basket of goods and services that could be bought for 72 pesos in December 2006 was worth 100 pesos in December 2014.

Did you know that...

The Cordillera region contributes bulk of the country’s cabbage production?

Data from the former Bureau of Agricultural Statistics reveal that the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR) dominated in cabbage production nationwide with a share of 78.4 percent in 2013. Central Visayas was a distant second at 6.7 percent followed by Northern Mindanao at 4.9 percent.

Did you know that...

13 out of every 100 police officers in the Cordilleras are women?

In 2013, the share of women to the total police force in the Cordilleras was only 13.1 percent or 507 officers. Most of these policewomen (93.5%) were non-commissioned officers while the rest (6.5%) were commissioned officers.

More than 40% of 2012 investments in CAR are in Baguio City?

The total value of investments in the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR) reached nearly 3.3 billion pesos in 2012 which generated a total of 24,905 jobs. Baguio City contributed the biggest share at 41.2% while Apayao accounted for the smallest share at 5.2%.

Did you know that...

Household expenditure accounted for most of the GRDE of the Cordilleras in 2012?

The 2012 Gross Regional Domestic Expenditure (GRDE) Report of the National Statistical Coordination Board reveals that Household Final Consumption Expenditure (HFCE) still comprise bulk or 60.8 percent of the GRDE of the Cordilleras. The region’s HFCE was Php 74.8 billion in 2012 at constant 2000 prices.

Did you know that...

Benguet posted the highest HDI in the Cordilleras in 2009?

The 2009 Human Development Index (HDI) revealed that people living in Benguet generally enjoy a better quality of life compared to the other five provinces in the region. In 2009, Benguet obtained an HDI of 0.883, the highest in the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR). The generation of the 2009 HDI is a joint effort of the National Statistical Coordination Board (NSCB) and the Human Development Network (HDN).

1 in every 2 mothers in CAR exclusively breastfeed their babies?

Nearly half (49.2%) of all mothers in the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR) practice exclusive breastfeeding and this is highest among the 17 regions. This is from the results of the 2011 Family Health Survey conducted by the National Statistics Office.

Did you know that...

Women live longer than men?

In the Cordillera Administrative Region, women outlive men by an average of 5.5 years for the period 2011-2015 and 2016-2020.


Among the provinces, Benguet has the longest life expectancy at 75.9 years for women and 70.6 years for men. Mt. Province has the widest gap of which women will live longer by 6.7 years than men.

Did you know that...

Only 6 out of 10 pupils in the Cordilleras completed elementary in 2009?

About 64.1 percent of the enrollees in SY 2009-2010 graduated from elementary in the Cordilleras. This is 13.7 percentage points lower than the primary completion rate recorded by DepEd in 1991.


Primary completion rate is defined as the percentage of first grade entrants in the elementary level who complete or finish the level in accordance with the required number of years of study.

Less women in CAR are using any modern family planning method?

Did you know that...

CAR recorded the highest casualties during typhoons Ondoy and Pepeng?

As of 5 November 2009, data from the National Disaster Coordinating Council (NDCC) reveal that CAR incurred a total of 384 casualties that comprised of 350 deaths and 34 missing - the highest among the Luzon regions hit by typhoons Ondoy and Pepeng. The tallies constitute 37.67% and 40.48% of the total Luzonwide deaths and persons missing, respectively. NCR follows with 241 deaths and Region IV-A with 160 deaths and 20 missing.

The Philippine National Police handled 325 criminal cases in the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR) from April to June 2008. Did you know that...

Benguet was among the least poor provinces nationwide in 2006 ?

On one hand, poverty incidence among families in Benguet province decreased from 11.0% to 8.2% between 2003 and 2006. Benguet’s nationwide ranking improved from the 9th to the 5th least poor province during the period. On the other hand, poverty incidence in Apayao more than tripled to reach 57.5% in 2006. Apayao joined the 10 poorest provinces of the country at rank 4 during said year.
