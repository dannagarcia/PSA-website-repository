CAR inflation rate unchanged in April 2014

The annual inflation rate for all items in the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR) remained steady at 2.7 percent between March and April 2014. At 1.4 percentage points (ppts.) below the national average, CARâ€™s inflation rate remained the lowest among the 17 regions in the country..

Among commodity groups, Education maintained the highest inflation rate at 8.4 percent, followed by Alcoholic Beverages and Tobacco at 6.0 percent and Furnishing, Household Equipment & Routine Maintenance of the House at 4.4 percent.

Inflation for the heavily weighted Food and Non-Alcoholic Beverages group eased by 0.1 percentage point while the Alcoholic Beverages and Tobacco slowed down by 1.8 ppts. Moreover, inflation declined by 0.2 ppt. in Health, Clothing & Footwear, and Furnishing, Household Equipment & Routine Maintenance of the House. Posting a downtick of 0.1 ppt each were the Recreation & Culture group and the Restaurants and Miscellaneous Goods and Services group.

An increment of 0.1 ppt. was registered by Housing, Water, Electricity, Gas, and Other Fuels. Inflation rates for Education and Communication were unchanged.

The purchasing power of the peso in CAR stood at 74 centavos as of April 2014
