February 2006

Did you know that...
One peso in 2005 is now worth only 73 centavos in the Cordillera?

The purchasing power of the peso in the Cordillera was pegged at 73 centavos as of December 2005. On the average, the same basket of goods and services worth P73 in 2000 could be bought for P100 last month.

CONSUMER PRICE INDEX (CPI) AND PURCHASING PRICE OF THE PESO (PPP) as of December 2005 (2000=100)
YEAR CPI PPP
Philippines 133.7 0.75
CAR 137.3 0.73
Source of Basic Data: National Statistics Office

March 2006

Did you know that...
there were more women Overseas Filipino Workers (OFW) than men in CAR?

In 2001, an estimated 15,000 or more than two-thirds (68.2%) of the total OFWs from CAR were women. In 2002, although the number of women OFWs from the region declined, their share to the total expanded to more than three-fourths (77.8%).

May 2006

Did you know that...
Revenue collections in CAR reached P3.6 billion in 2005?

In 2004, taxpayers in the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR) paid more than P2 billion to the Bureau of Internal Revenue. In 2005, revenue collections expanded by 78.0 percent to reach P3.6 billion. Baguio City taxpayers paid the lion's share at P2.9 billion or 80 percent of total regional collection.

September 2006

Did you know that...
Only 6 out of every 10 babies born in CAR are protected against neonatal tetanus?

The 2005 Family Planning and Maternal and Child Health Survey of the National Statistics Office reveals that 61.5 percent of children below 5 years in Cordillera were protected at birth against neonatal tetanus. CARAGA has the least percent of children provided with the tetanus toxoid vaccine among all regions of the country.

Area Percent
Philippines 66.8
CAR 61.5
CARAGA 48.5
Source of Basic Data: National Statistics Office

November 2006

Did you know that...
9 out of every 10 tourists in the Cordilleras are domestic travelers?

As per the Department of Tourism (DOT), majority or 91.1 percent of tourists who visited the Cordillera region in year 2005 were domestic or local travelers. Foreign visitors accounted for 8.2 percent while visiting overseas Filipinos or “balikbayans” were the minority at 0.7 percent.
