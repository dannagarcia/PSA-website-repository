14 Out of Every 100 Elected Official in CAR is a Woman

In the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR), women occupy only 14.4 percent or 120 of the 833 elective positions for the 2004-2007 term of office. Men still hold majority at 83.8 percent or 698 positions from Congressman down to the Municipal or City Councilor.

Of the total number of elected women officials, 91.6 percent or 110 women serve in the legislative branch while only 8.3 percent or 10 serve in the executive branch of the local government. At the local level, the legislative branch includes the positions of Congressman, Vice-Governor, Board Member, Vice Mayor, and Councilor. The local executive branch includes the positions of Governor and Mayor.

During the same period there were no women candidates in the region elected to the positions of Congressman, Governor and Vice Governor.
