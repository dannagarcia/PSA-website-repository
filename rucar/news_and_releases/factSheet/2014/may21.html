Six out of ten college enrollees in CAR are women

More women than men enroll in Higher Education Institutions (HEIs) in the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR). Women comprised more than half or an average of 57.5 percent of all college enrollees in the region from 2008 to 2013 according to data from the Commission on Higher Education in CAR.

The gap between men and women in college enrollment in the region shrank in 2009 but has since widened in favor of women. In 2013, Benguet had the widest gender gap with 179 women for every 100 men enrollees in the province. Kalinga came next with 165 women to 100 men and Ifugao followed with 157 women to 100 men. Abra recorded the least gender gap with 122 women for every 100 men enrollees for the same period.

Baguio City continues to be the center of tertiary education in CAR with 21 HEIs or 37.5 percent of the regional total. HEIs in Baguio include 2 state-run institutions namely the University of the Philippines - Baguio and the Philippine Military Academy and 19 private schools including Saint Louis University, University of Baguio and University of the Cordilleras.
