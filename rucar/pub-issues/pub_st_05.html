Only 14 out of every 100 elected official in CAR is a woman

March 2005 Issue

In the Cordilleras, women occupy only 120 or 14.4 percent of all elective seats for the 2004-2007 term of office. There is no woman Representative, Governor or Vice Governor in the region.

Revenue collections in CAR reached P3.6 billion in 2005

May 2005 Issue

IIn 2004, taxpayers in the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR) paid more than P2 billion to the Bureau of Internal Revenue. In 2005, revenue collections expanded by 78.0 percent to reach P3.6 billion. Baguio City taxpayers paid the lion's share at P2.9 billion or 80 percent of total regional collection.

Did you know that...
One peso in 2000 is now worth only 76 centavos in the Cordillera?

The purchasing power of the peso in the Cordillera was pegged at 76 centavos as of June 2005. On the average, the same basket of goods and services worth P76 in 2000 could be bought for P100 last month .

Women dominate men in functional literacy in the Cordilleras

December 2005

The 2003 Functional Literacy, Education and Mass Media Survey (FLEMMS) of the National Statistics Office reveals that the female functional literacy rate in CAR (87.0 percent) was 3.1 percentage points higher than males (83.9 percent). Functional literacy involves basic reading, writing and computing skills.
